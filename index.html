Introduction

Farmers without small fortunes for expensive machinery are faced with the challenge of manually refilling animal water troughs. Considering how large the animal headcount on a farm can be, this manual process can waste a lot time that could be better spent doing more productive work. Additionally, there are facts presented by the patterns of water taken by animals on a regular basis, facts which remain unseen because farmers are unable to collect the data needed to identify these patterns. As a solution to this problem, we propose an embedded system specifically designed to automatically refill the animal water troughs, track how much water is taken regularly and uniquely identify which animals have taken water and by elimination which animals haven’t.

Methodology

Water Level Tracking

The system tracks the level water in the rough in real time using an ultrasonic sensor. The ultrasonic sensor uses a transducer to send and receive ultrasonic pulses that relay back information about an object’s proximity (the surface of the water, in this case). It sends an ultrasonic pulse which travels through the air and if there is an obstacle or object, it will bounce back to the sensor. By calculating the travel time and the speed of sound, the distance can be calculated. And from this distance, the water level can be tracked

Automatic Water Supply

With the water level tracking functionality, the system is able to tell when the tank is full and when it is empty. When the tank is empty, the system passes current through the brushless water pump to automatically refill the trough by pumping water from the water source (tank). When the tank is full on refilling it the water supply is cut off by stopping the current through the brushless water pump.

Animal Identification

When an animal comes to take water from the trough, the RFID reader detects the RFID tags attached to the animal. The RFID tags have unique tags which are read by the RFID reader to uniquely identify the animal that is presently taking water.

Server Data Storage

The system uses a GSM module and its GPRS functionality to send collected data to the server hosted by thingsspeak.com.

Mobile Application Statistics

A mobile application client requests for statistics information from the server and displays it to the user. The application shows information like daily water intake, cows that haven’t taken water and daily number of refills.

Objectives

  • To automatically refill the animal water trough when it is empty.
  • To track how much water is taken by the animals on a regular basis.
  • To identify which animals are taking water.
  • To send all the relevant data collected to a server that composes useful statistics.
  • To show the farmer useful statistics through a client mobile application that exposes this information from the server.
